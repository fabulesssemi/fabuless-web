TITLE: Google Gemini Eats The World – Gemini Smashes GPT-4 By 5X, The GPU-Poors
SOURCE: SemiAnalysis - Dylan Patel
DATE: 2023-08-27
URL: https://www.semianalysis.com/p/google-gemini-eats-the-world

Google is iterating at a pace that will smash GPT-4 total pre-training FLOPS by 5x before end of 2023, with a clear path to 20x by end of 2024. Gemini training began on TPUv5 (Viperfish) clusters.

Google's Compute Advantage

Excluding entire existing TPUv4 fleet and TPUv5e (inference workhorse), Google's TPUv5 (Viperfish) ramp alone represents more quarterly chip additions than OpenAI's entire projected GPU fleet growth over 2 years. TPUv5 pod: 8,960 chips connected via 800G optical circuit switches in multi-pod configurations. Google has 100k+ TPUv5 chips in production by end of 2023 just for Gemini training — conservatively.

Google AI Infrastructure Supremacy (detailed in prior report): Google achieves better performance/TCO by using 3-5x more chips at lower Capex per chip (no Nvidia margin). Gemini infrastructure uses multi-pod TPUv5 connected via OCS for reconfigurable training topology. OCS enables any-to-any GPU connectivity within 4ms — critical for pipeline parallelism across pods.

GPU-Rich vs GPU-Poor

GPU-Rich firms: OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, Inflection, xAI, Meta. Each researcher at these firms has access to thousands of GPUs for experiments. Meta is using "we have the 2nd most H100s in the world" as a recruiting tactic.

GPU-Poor firms: HuggingFace, Databricks/MosaicML, Together.ai. World-class researchers, but orders of magnitude fewer GPUs than needed to train competitive frontier models. Fundamental problem: must build before customers come, but cannot fundraise enough to match Nvidia DGX Cloud service or hyperscaler infrastructure.

GPU-Poor problems:
- Fine-tuning small models on broken leaderboards (style over substance evaluations)
- Ignoring MoE architectures and speculative decoding used by all frontier labs
- Training on too-small datasets with inadequate quality
- Obsessing over quantization/memory constraints instead of efficiency at scale

HuggingFace: too-high valuation (~$4.5B) prevents raising the capital needed to compete. Leaderboards are "hurting the open source movement" by creating useless benchmark-optimized models.

Databricks/MosaicML: $1.3B acquisition was a big bet. Need to raise 4x more capital for infrastructure. Could capture enterprise fine-tuning market but needs to outspend Nvidia's DGX Cloud service.

Together.ai: best inference engine of the "race to bottom" group but fundamentally GPU-poor.

Nvidia DGX Cloud

Nvidia is building their own cloud service with enterprise AI customers: Amgen, Adobe, CCC, ServiceNow, Accenture, AstraZeneca, Getty Images, Shutterstock, Morningstar, plus undisclosed partnerships. Offers pre-trained models, fine-tuning frameworks, vector databases, inference APIs, and Nvidia expert support. "Everyone from Meta to Microsoft to startups is a pipeline of capital to Nvidia's bank account."

Open Source Landscape

GPU-poors are using dense LLAMA models (Meta's gift) despite MoE being more efficient. Without the incentive to be efficient on 10k+ GPU systems, open source researchers are solving the wrong problems. European startups and government supercomputers are "completely uncompetitive" — Jules Verne cluster is GPU-poor relative to US and China private investment. Saudi Arabia and UAE investing more in AI infrastructure than European governments.

Can Google Save Us from Nvidia?

Google is the only viable Nvidia alternative at scale. TPUv5 brings Gemini to competitive parity or ahead of GPT-4. Google AI Infrastructure Supremacy (systems over microarchitecture) enables 2x better performance/TCO than Nvidia. The question is whether Google has product and distribution execution to capitalize — Gemini launch will answer this.

Investment Implication

The GPU-rich/GPU-poor divide is widening and will not close. Frontier model training is a compute race, and the winners are locked in. For investors, the GPU-poor "picks and shovels" plays (HuggingFace, Together, Cohere) are structurally disadvantaged. The real picks-and-shovels are Nvidia (obvious), Broadcom (Google TPU), and TSMC/SK Hynix (manufacturing). Google's Gemini marks the end of OpenAI's unopposed frontier model leadership.

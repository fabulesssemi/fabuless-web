TITLE: AI Capacity Constraints - CoWoS and HBM Supply Chain
SOURCE: SemiAnalysis - Dylan Patel
DATE: 2023-07-05
URL: https://www.semianalysis.com/p/ai-capacity-constraints-cowos-hbm

H100 will remain sold out until Q1 2024 despite Nvidia ramping to 400,000 H100 GPUs per quarter. The binding constraints are CoWoS packaging and HBM supply — not GPU wafer production.

Why Wafer Supply Is Not the Constraint

TSMC N5 utilization fell below 70% due to weakness in PC, smartphone, and non-AI datacenter chips. Nvidia has no problem securing N4 wafer supply. Nvidia is running wafer bank strategy — ordering wafers ahead of downstream CoWoS capacity, holding at TSMC as die bank WIP. GPU dice are already fabbed; they wait in queue for CoWoS packaging.

HBM: SK Hynix Dominance

SK Hynix: sole volume supplier of HBM3 in 2023, >95% market share. HBM3 started production June 2022. Current max configuration: 8-layer 16GB modules (5 active stacks on H100). SK Hynix ramping 12-layer 24GB HBM3 at 5.6 GT/s for AMD MI300X and Nvidia H100 refresh.

Samsung: shipping HBM3 from H2 2023. Cutting favorable pricing deals to capture market share. Showing roadmap for 12-layer HBM and future hybrid-bonded HBM4 (base die on in-house FinFET node).

Micron: stuck on HBM2E, cannot manufacture top-bin at high volume. Bold claims of HBM3E leadership in CQ1 2024 — SemiAnalysis skeptical given current execution gap. Micron historically invested in Hybrid Memory Cube (HMC, failed) rather than HBM until 2018 pivot.

AMD invented HBM (partnership with SK Hynix in 2008, first shipped with Fiji GPU in 2015) but Nvidia and Google are now the highest volume HBM users.

CoWoS: The Real Bottleneck

TSMC's CoWoS capacity: only advanced packaging technology in high volume for AI accelerators. CoWoS-S is the workhorse — logic die + HBM on silicon interposer. TSMC's Advanced Backend Fab 6 in Zhunan: 14.3 hectares cleanroom space (larger than all other TSMC packaging fabs combined). Rush tooling orders to equipment makers. Still cannot keep up with demand through Q1 2024.

Alternatives being explored:
- Amkor: Nvidia secured Amkor capacity for H100 production — first diversification beyond TSMC for advanced AI packaging
- JCET: working on CoWoS-equivalent technology
- ASE: FOEB (Fan-Out Embedded Bridge) competing technology, sourcing silicon interposer from UMC

HBM and CoWoS Together

HBM2E: 307 GB/s bandwidth per stack, 8-Hi or 4-Hi configuration. Used in A100.
HBM3: 819 GB/s per stack (2x HBM2E). Used in H100.
HBM3E: ~1,200 GB/s per stack. Used in H200, MI300X, H100 refresh.
HBM4: target >2,000 GB/s per stack. Base die on TSMC leading node. Hybrid bonded stacking. Roadmap 2025-2026.

Each H100 uses 5 active HBM3 stacks (1 dummy die for mechanical balance). Total HBM content per H100: ~80GB at 3.35 TB/s. Nvidia has pre-purchased majority of SK Hynix HBM allocation, crowding out AMD, Google/Broadcom (except for contracted shares).

Customer Volume Estimates (by accelerator)

SemiAnalysis proprietary database covers quarterly volumes for: Nvidia H100/H800/A100, Broadcom/Google TPU, AMD MI300/MI250, Amazon Trainium2/Inferentia, Marvell/Microsoft Athena, Alchip/META MTIA, Alibaba T-Head, ZTE Sanechips, Samsung Mach-1. Available to clients.

Key finding: total CoWoS wafer capacity through H1 2024 is fully allocated. TSMC has no unallocated slots. Any new customer wanting CoWoS capacity is on a waitlist for H2 2024+.

Investment Implication

CoWoS is the most important supply chain chokepoint in AI infrastructure. TSMC's backend capacity expansion capex is the most directly traceable AI-driven investment in the equipment supply chain. Equipment vendors enabling CoWoS and HBM production (DISCO, AMAT, Lam, KLA, TEL) are structural beneficiaries through 2025. SK Hynix has a 12-18 month lead on HBM quality — Samsung is second but cutting deals to close the volume gap. Micron is a distant third with upside if HBM3E execution delivers.
